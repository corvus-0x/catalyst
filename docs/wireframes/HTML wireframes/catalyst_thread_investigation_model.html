Thread-based investigation model: knot view and thread pulling workspace with AI suggestions

The investigation is a knot with threads coming off it. You pull one thread at a time. Multiple documents feed one thread. The AI watches what you've gathered and tells you where to pull next. When a thread is exhausted, you tie it off and go back to the knot.
The knot
The central entity the investigation orbits — usually a person or org. Karen Homan is the knot in Do Good. Every thread starts and ends here. Returning to the knot means stepping back from the detail and asking: what else does this person connect to that I haven't pulled yet?

A thread
One line of inquiry. Starts as a question. Accumulates evidence from multiple documents. Gets an AI suggestion for what to look at next. Ends in one of three states:
Pulling — active, still gathering evidence
Confirmed — enough evidence, tied off as a finding
Exhausted — pulled as far as it goes, no more evidence found
Why threads change the data model
Old model (broken)
Document → Finding. One finding has one trigger document. If three documents together prove the same pattern, you end up with three separate findings that all look unrelated, or you force one document to "own" the finding and the others are footnotes. The referral package looks weak because the connections are invisible.
Thread model (correct)
Thread ← Documents (many). The 990 Part VII, the building permit, and the AOS audit all feed the same thread: "Karen Homan is being compensated off-book through Baumer Construction." The thread is the finding. The three documents are its citations. The referral package presents the full chain, not isolated data points.
What changes in the Investigate tab
Left rail
Documents list stays. Below it: Threads list (active threads + their status). Clicking a thread opens Thread view.
Center panel
Two modes: Document viewer (reading a doc) or Thread view (working a thread — shows evidence gathered + what to do next).
Right panel
When reading a doc: shows which threads this doc feeds into. When on a thread: shows AI suggestions for what to pull next + "tie off" button.

Tie off options
"Confirm as finding" creates a Finding record with all three documents as citations and auto-fills the evidence snapshot.

Haiku and Sonnet play different roles. Haiku extracts — it pulls facts from documents. Sonnet reasons — it looks at the thread you're building and points to the next pull. Neither one writes the finding. You do.

Haiku — document extraction
Runs automatically when a document is uploaded and OCR'd. Pulls: entities (persons, orgs, EINs, addresses), financial figures, dates, form field values. Surfaces these as candidate evidence items on the relevant thread. Does not reason about what they mean.
Example: reads the 990 Part VII, extracts "Karen Homan — $0 compensation" as a candidate fact. Adds it to the hidden compensation thread if one exists, or flags it as unassigned evidence.
Sonnet — thread reasoning
Runs when you open a thread or click "What should I look at next?" Reads the evidence you've gathered so far on that thread and suggests: what document to find, what search to run, what connection to check. It knows what you already have and doesn't repeat it.
Example: sees you have $0 officer pay + Baumer permit + $48K uncompetitive payment. Suggests: check Ohio SOS for Baumer entity registration, look for Homan as member or agent.
What the AI never does
Does not write the finding narrative — the investigator writes what was found, in plain fact language, with citations
Does not assign evidence weight — the investigator decides how proven a thread is
Does not tie off threads — only a human can decide a thread is confirmed or exhausted. AI suggests; investigator decides.
Thread lifecycle with AI involvement
1
Investigator opens a thread — writes the question: "Is Homan being paid off-book through Baumer?"
H
Haiku extracts from each document as it's added to the thread — surfaces candidate facts automatically
3
Investigator confirms or rejects each extracted fact — Haiku is fast but not infallible, human stays in the loop
S
Sonnet reasons about what's been confirmed so far and suggests the next pull — which document to find, which search to run
5
Investigator pulls the next document based on Sonnet's suggestion — or ignores it and follows their own instinct
6
Thread is tied off when the investigator decides — confirmed (finding) or exhausted (dead end). AI does not decide this.
